AstropStudy welcomes you to the Solar System!

Have you ever stopped to think about what exists beyond what is in our atmosphere? Well, when we talk about the Solar System we are definitely not talking about everything that happens in our Universe. But yes, a small part of it all, a clipping of what revolves around the Sun, our star.

The Solar System is made up of eight planets, dwarf planets, dozens of natural satellites, thousands of asteroids, meteors, meteoroids and comets that revolve around a single massive point that corresponds to the gravitational center of it all, the Sun. We are located in one of the many galaxies in the Universe, the Milky Way.




Origin of the Solar System

The Solar System is estimated to have formed about 4.7 billion years ago. Its origin is still uncertain, however, the theory most accepted by the scientific community today is the solar nebula theory, initially proposed by René Descartes in 1644, being reformulated by Immanuel Kant in 1775 and, later, by Pierre-Simon de Laplace in 1796.

The theory assumes that the Sun was formed from the rotation of this cloud which, when contracting under the influence of gravity, increased its speed, then collapsed. Thus, the Sun formed due to the central concentration of the nebula and the planets formed from the remnants of the collapsing molecular cloud.




The Planets of the Solar System

Currently, the Solar System consists of 8 planets, namely:

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.
